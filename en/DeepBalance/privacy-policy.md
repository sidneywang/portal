# Privacy Policy

**Last Updated**: June 10, 2026

DeepBalance+ (hereinafter referred to as "this App") respects and protects the privacy of all users. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect your information.

## 1. Information Collection

This App does not collect any personally identifiable information.

- This App does not require an account or login. We do not collect your name, email address, phone number, or any other personally identifiable information.
- This App does not collect usage data. We do not track how many times you open the App, how long you use it, or your click behavior.
- This App does not collect location information and does not request any location permissions.

## 2. Use of Information

Since this App does not collect any information, there is no use, analysis, sharing, or sale of your personal data.

## 3. Local Permissions

To provide its core functionality (checking DeepSeek balance from the menu bar), this App may request the following system permissions:

| Permission | Purpose | Required |
|---|---|---|
| Notifications / Widget | To display balance info in the widget | No |

You can revoke these permissions at any time in System Settings. If you disable them, the widget feature will stop working, but other features will remain unaffected.

## 4. Third-Party Services

This App does not integrate any third-party SDKs (such as Umeng, Firebase, Google Analytics, etc.) and does not send any data to third parties.

## 5. Data Storage

- Your API key is stored locally in the App's shared container on your device and is never uploaded to any server.
- The App queries balance via DeepSeek's official API (api.deepseek.com). Your API key is only passed in the request header and is not logged or stored.

## 6. Children's Privacy

This App does not knowingly collect any information from users of any age, including children under 13.

## 7. Changes to This Privacy Policy

If this Privacy Policy changes, we will notify you by updating this document in a future App update. The revised policy will take effect on the date of publication.
